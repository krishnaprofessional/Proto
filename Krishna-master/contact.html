What Happens in Blood NTR Blood Bank

The path of the blood from blood donor to a fellow human being contains several steps. It should be tested, centrifuged, divided and then given to the one who needs it. I have listed all those steps below.

The Donation

1. You arrive for your blood donation appointment.

2. Health history and mini physical tests are completed.

3. For a whole blood donation, about 1 pint of blood is collected, several small test tubes of blood are also collected for testing.

4. Your donation, test tubes and your donor record are labeled with an identical bar code label.

5. Your donation is kept on ice before being taken to a centre for processing, the test tubes go to the lab.

Processing

1. Most whole blood donations are spun in centrifuges to separate it into transfusable components: red cells, platelets, and plasma.

2. Plasma may be processed into components such as cryoprecipitate, which helps control the risk of bleeding by helping blood to clot.

3. Red cells and platelets are leuko-reduced, which means your white cells are removed in order to reduce the possibility of the recipient having a reaction to the transfusion.

4. Each component is packaged as a “unit,” a standardised amount that doctors will use when transfusing a patient.

Testing

1. In parallel with above step, your test tubes arrive at a testing laboratory.

2. A dozen tests are performed, to establish the blood type and test for infectious diseases

3. Test results are transferred electronically to the processing center within 24 hours.

4. If a test result is positive, your donation will be discarded and you will be notified
