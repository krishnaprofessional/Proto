EMPATHISE

The first step of my process is to provide an opportunity to set my assumptions aside and immerse in the context of the problem I attempting to remedy. I have several approaches that can help me draw out the information needed to understand the problem’s context, But depending available options and design time line, I preferred following approaches to understand about how people are currently sharing blood donations data and logistics between hospitals and also how they schedule for future requests based on surgeries. Asking someone to tell a story about the last time they experienced the problem you’re investigating provides a rich description that highlights details you might not have otherwise considered.

Competative Research

Competitive evaluation is often a forgotten & underwhelming step in the UX Process. However, I use Molecule as a framing model developed by janice fraser. The molecule acts as a guide to answer three questions.

  • Who are your User?
  • What problem are they struggling with?
  • How is you’re offering going to solve it?

This model also acts as our guide to our competitive research

I picked European developed countries health care system as competitors, as their user profile matches with our product requirements.

Listing out Comparative Modules and Evaluation Criterias

First important step is to know product domain, which I have done in Literature Review and start by running a “self-analysis” to understand which modules and criteria we need to compare and benchmark with our competitors.

  • Testing Eligibility
  • Checking Requirements
  • Accepting Requests
  • Learnability
  • Efficiency
  • Memorability
  • ErrorManagement
  • Satisfaction

After analysing every criteria for each competitor, I want to organise this data in a “competitive analysis table” that should give us a clear picture of best practices in current domain.

Define Personas

From data I gathered from Literature Review, Competitive Review, SMEs interviews, I tried to understand entire task performed in system, Later I tried to identify task owners. Then shortlisted our target users and started defining there User profile, Task profile, Journey, Environmental Profile, Goals, Behaviour, Important people, Pain Points.

System Flow

1) Blood Collection
2) Component Separation
3) Quarantine Storage
4) Screening
5) Stored in Screened Storage
6) Discard Blood
7) Request from Hospital
8) Cross Matching patient sample blood to find a match.
9) Issued to patient.

Identifying Key Profiles in System Flow

1) Nurse - Collects Blood
2) Donor - Gives Blood
3) Medical Technician - Separates Blood
4) Medical Laboratory Technologist - Tests blood and gives output
5) Medical Laboratory Technical Supervisor - Approves Tests and runs Laboratory
6) Clinical Laboratory Manager - Approves and manages entire operation.
7) Hospital Manager - Schedules Blood requests
8) Doctors - Send patients requirement to hospital manager
9) Patient - Need of Blood
