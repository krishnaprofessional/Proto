Interview Setting: Interview conducted in office of NTR Blood bank building. The interview was conducted at 10:30 AM.

Interview Starts

Can I know about your self?

My name is Shesider. I am Technical Manager and project management professional with over 11 years in leadership and project manager roles. I have experience, developing clear project roadmaps from Blood campaigns to Approving and issuing it to patients in Hospitals.

As we sit before your desktop computer, would you walk me through the process that happens in NTR Blood bank?

“1) Blood Collection - Unique ID (5 STRAPS)
2) Component Seperation - PRBC, FFP, CRYO, RDP (5 TO 8 HOURS)
3) Quarantine Storage - PRBC, FFP, CRYO, RDP
4) Screening - HIV, HBS, HCV, VDRC, MP, Blood grouping
5) Stored in Screened Storage
6) Discard Blood
7) Request from Hospital
8) Cross Matching patient sample blood to find a match ( FIFO )
9) Issued to patient.“ - started writing this on a book explained this to me.

Who are key players in this process and what are their responsibility?

1) Nurse - Collects Blood
2) Donor - Gives Blood
3) Medical Technician - Separates Blood
4) Medical Laboratory Technologist - Tests blood and gives output
5) Medical Laboratory Technical Supervisor - Approves Tests and runs Laboratory
6) Clinical Laboratory Manager 1 - Approves and manages entire operation.
7) Hospital Manager - Schedules Blood requests
8) Doctors - Send patients requirement to hospital manager
9) Patient - Need of Blood

Can I know about data points and where do you gather them?

1) Bag type - depending upon requirements
2) Profile data - Donor Form
3) Unique ID allotment
4) Seperation ID
5) Test Results
7) Approved Test Results
8) Patient Requirement data
9) Cross match results
10) Delivery details

How do you collect this blood?

65% through campaigns
30% donor schedules and donates blood in blood banks
5% others

How do you separate components?

Blood donations are spun in centrifuges to separate it into transfusable components

What are separated components and usage?

RDP (Random Donor Platelets) form clots to stop bleeding.
PRBC (Packed red blood cells) used to transport oxygen throughout body
FFP (Fresh frozen plasma) transport blood cells throughout your body.

How do you store them?

Quarantine Storage -
PRBC (2°c to 8°c)
FFP (-40°c)
RDP (22°c)

When do you test them?

Within a day.

What tests you perform on them?

Our Tests -
HIV, HBS, HCV, VDRC, MP, Blood grouping (35 to 45 min) Automatic & Manual
Hepatitis B virus (HBV) - Automation
Hepatitis C virus (HCV) - Automation
Human Immunodeficiency viruses (HIV) - Automation
Syphilis (VDRL) - Automation
Malarial parasite (MP) - Rapid Diagnostic Test (RDT)
Blood Grouping -
In addition to these tests, every unit is tested to identify the donor’s blood group (O, A, B or AB) and Rh type and screened for a typical or unusual red cell antibodies.
Other tests:
Trypanosoma cruzi (anti-T. cruzi)
Human T-Lymphotropic virus (HTLV-I/II)
Zika virus NAT
West Nile virus (NAT)
Babesiosis (Babesia microti) Antibody and NAT

What time do you perform this test?

We don’t have any specific time for this

What is max time taken to perform this tests?

35 min for Automation Tests
40 min for Rapid Diagnostic Test (RDT)

What is max time taken to perform this tests?

One hour

Where do you store them?

In Screened Storage - Categorised and organised

What is the ideal temperature for storage?

Quarantine Storage -
PRBC (2°c to 8°c)
FFP (-40°c)
RDP (22°c)

What about expiry dates?

RDP (Random Donor Platelets) Expiry: 5 days
PRBC (Packed red blood cells) Expiry: 42 days.
FFP (Fresh frozen plasma) Expiry: 1 year.

What is discard blood and at what situation you consider blood has to be discarded?

If blood donated by donor is
Expired
Leaked
Test Positive
Then blood will no longer be used for patients, so this units will be Discarded.

How do hospital manager contact you?

Mostly Phone Call.

What do you do in cross matching?

Sample of Patient Blood is sent to Blood bank
Blood is tested and possible match is found.
Deliverables are cross checked again and sent to Manager for approval

Can I know about pain points you face?

- Many parts in the country still have problem of too few voluntary donors.
- The other problem is that blood donation camps do not happen all year round and the blood banks face periodic shortage.
- Lack of uniform, consistent and up-to-date regulation and policies from the Government with the Department of Drugs and Cosmetics Standard.
- Struggle to compete with blood banks running with commercial motives which tend to influence the organisers of camps.
- No centralised database.
- Inaccessibility to available blood in case of requirements.
- Lack of proper instruments at Blood Bank centres.

What is max units of blood requirements?

Incase of surgeries, patient may require 100 units of blood

If you have a wand that can change everything, what will you change?

I want a system which can simplify routine steps and solve above problems
